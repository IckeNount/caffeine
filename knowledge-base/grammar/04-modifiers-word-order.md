# Adjectives & Modifiers: Word Order Differences

## Overview
The most fundamental difference between English and Thai grammar: In English, adjectives come BEFORE the noun. In Thai, adjectives come AFTER the noun. This reversal is the core of "Thai Logic" in LinguBreak.

## The Core Rule

### English: Adjective + Noun
- **big** house → บ้าน**ใหญ่** (house + big)
- **beautiful** girl → สาว**สวย** (girl + beautiful)
- **red** car → รถ**สีแดง** (car + color-red)

### Thai: Noun + Adjective
The adjective always follows the noun it modifies.

## Multiple Adjectives

### English: Specific order (opinion → size → age → shape → color → origin → material)
- "a **beautiful large old round red Italian wooden** table"
- (Thai students rarely encounter all 7, but 2-3 modifiers are common)

### Thai: Noun + adjectives (but order is more flexible)
- โต๊ะ**ไม้อิตาลีสีแดงกลมเก่าใหญ่สวย**
- In practice, Thai puts the most important modifier first

## Compound Modifiers

### English: Uses hyphens or compound structures
- "a **well-known** professor" → อาจารย์**ที่มีชื่อเสียง** (professor + ที่ + famous)
- "a **five-year-old** child" → เด็ก**อายุห้าขวบ** (child + age-five-years)
- "an **English-speaking** country" → ประเทศ**ที่พูดภาษาอังกฤษ** (country + ที่ + speak English)

Note: Compound modifiers in English often become relative clauses (ที่) in Thai.

## Participial Adjectives (-ing / -ed)

### English: Present participle (-ing) and past participle (-ed) as adjectives
- "The **boring** movie" (the movie causes boredom)
- "The **bored** student" (the student feels boredom)
- "The **running** water" (water that is running)
- "The **broken** window" (window that was broken)

### Thai: Uses different constructions
- หนัง**น่าเบื่อ** (movie + น่า-boring) — uses น่า prefix for -ing adjectives
- นักเรียน**ที่เบื่อ** (student + ที่ + bored) — uses ที่ for -ed adjectives
- น้ำ**ที่ไหล** (water + ที่ + flow)
- หน้าต่าง**ที่แตก** (window + ที่ + broken)

## Chunking Rules for Modifiers
- Adjective(s) + the noun they modify = consider as related
- But in chunking: adjectives that appear BEFORE the noun should be labeled `modifier`
- In Thai reordering: move the modifier AFTER its noun
- Label: `modifier`
- Color: Purple (bg-purple-100)

## Adverbs
Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs:
- "She speaks English **fluently**" → เธอพูดภาษาอังกฤษ**ได้คล่อง**
- "He **quickly** finished" → เขาทำเสร็จ**อย่างรวดเร็ว**
- Adverbs should be chunked with their verb or as separate `modifier` chunks

## Common Thai Student Mistakes
1. **Placing adjectives after nouns in English**: "house big" instead of "big house"
2. **Confusing -ing/-ed adjectives**: "I am boring" instead of "I am bored"
3. **Omitting articles with adjectives**: "She is beautiful girl" instead of "She is a beautiful girl"
4. **Word-for-word translation**: น่าสนใจ becomes "interesting to" instead of just "interesting"
