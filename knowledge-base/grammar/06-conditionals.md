# Conditionals: if/when/unless Patterns

## Overview
Conditional sentences express hypothetical situations and their consequences. English has a rigid system of conditional tenses. Thai conditionals are simpler — they mostly use ถ้า (thâa) and don't change verb tenses the same way.

## The 4 Conditionals

### Zero Conditional (General Truth)
- English: "If you **heat** water to 100°C, it **boils**." (present + present)
- Thai: ถ้าคุณต้มน้ำถึง 100°C น้ำจะเดือด
- Note: Thai uses จะ (jà = will) even for general truths

### First Conditional (Real Future)
- English: "If it **rains** tomorrow, I **will stay** home." (present + will)
- Thai: ถ้าพรุ่งนี้ฝนตก ฉันจะอยู่บ้าน
- Direct mapping, relatively easy for Thai students

### Second Conditional (Unreal Present)
- English: "If I **were** rich, I **would buy** a house." (past + would)
- Thai: ถ้าฉันรวย ฉันจะซื้อบ้าน — NO tense change!
- This is where Thai students struggle: English uses past tense for unreal present

### Third Conditional (Unreal Past)
- English: "If I **had studied** harder, I **would have passed**." (past perfect + would have)
- Thai: ถ้าฉันเรียนหนักกว่านี้ ฉันคงสอบผ่าน — minimal tense marking
- Most confusing for Thai students because English stacks 3 tense markers

## Key Difference: Thai Doesn't Change Verb Forms for Conditionals
English changes verb tenses to signal real vs. unreal:
- "If I **am** rich..." (real, possible)
- "If I **were** rich..." (unreal, hypothetical)
- "If I **had been** rich..." (unreal past)

Thai uses the SAME verb form but adds context words:
- ถ้าฉันรวย... (if I rich — same verb regardless)
- ถ้าตอนนั้นฉันรวย... (if at-that-time I rich — adds time word)

## Chunking Rules for Conditionals
- "If" + its clause = ONE chunk, labeled `modifier`
- The main clause (consequence) = separate chunk(s) with normal SVO labeling
- When fronted: "If..., [main clause]" — the if-clause is a sentence-level modifier
- When after: "[Main clause] if..." — same labeling

## Mixed Conditionals
- "If I **had studied** medicine, I **would be** a doctor now."
- Past condition → present result
- Thai: ถ้าฉันเรียนหมอ ตอนนี้ฉันคงเป็นหมอ

## Common Thai Student Mistakes
1. **Using "will" in the if-clause**: "If I will go..." instead of "If I go..."
2. **No tense change for unreal conditions**: "If I am rich..." instead of "If I were rich..."
3. **Confusing would/will**: Using "will" for hypothetical situations
4. **Skipping "have" in third conditional**: "If I studied harder" instead of "If I had studied harder"
