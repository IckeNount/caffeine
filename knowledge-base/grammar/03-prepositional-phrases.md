# Prepositional Phrases: in/on/at/by/with/from/to

## Overview
Prepositional phrases are modifiers that add information about time, place, manner, or reason. They are extremely common in English and cause confusion for Thai students because Thai prepositions work differently and are sometimes omitted entirely.

## Key Differences

### English: Prepositions are required and specific
- "I live **in** Bangkok" (in = inside a city)
- "I live **on** Sukhumvit Road" (on = along a road)
- "I live **at** 123 Main St" (at = specific address)

### Thai: Prepositions are often omitted or use ที่ as a catch-all
- ฉันอยู่กรุงเทพ (I live Bangkok — no preposition needed)
- ฉันอยู่**ที่**กรุงเทพ (I live **at** Bangkok — ที่ covers in/on/at)
- ฉันอยู่**บน**ถนนสุขุมวิท (I live **on** Sukhumvit Road — บน for "on")

## Common Preposition Mappings

| English | Thai | Usage |
|---------|------|-------|
| in | ใน (nai) | inside containers, countries, months, years |
| on | บน (bon) | surfaces, days, dates |
| at | ที่ (thîi) | specific locations, times |
| by | โดย (doi) | agent, method |
| with | กับ (kàp) / ด้วย (dûai) | accompaniment / instrument |
| from | จาก (jàak) | origin, source |
| to | ไป (bpai) / ถึง (thǔeng) | direction / destination |
| for | สำหรับ (sǎm-ràp) / เพื่อ (phûea) | purpose / beneficiary |
| about | เกี่ยวกับ (gìao-gàp) | topic |

## Prepositional Phrases as Modifiers

### Position in English: Flexible but usually after the noun/verb
- "The book **on the table** is mine." (after noun)
- "She works **at a hospital**." (after verb)
- "**In the morning**, I exercise." (fronted for emphasis)

### Position in Thai: Usually after the verb
- หนังสือ**บนโต๊ะ** เป็นของฉัน (book + on table = mine)
- เธอทำงาน**ที่โรงพยาบาล** (she + work + at hospital)
- **ตอนเช้า** ฉันออกกำลังกาย (morning + I + exercise)

## Chunking Rules for Prepositional Phrases
- Preposition + its entire object = ONE chunk
- Examples: "on the table", "by the professor", "in the morning"
- Label: `prepositional`
- Color: Amber (bg-amber-100)
- If the prepositional phrase modifies a noun, keep it with or adjacent to that noun in Thai reordering
- If it modifies a verb, place it after the verb in Thai order

## Stacked Prepositional Phrases
English often chains multiple prepositional phrases:
- "The student **from Thailand** **in my class** **with the red backpack**"
- Each prepositional phrase = separate chunk
- Thai order: นักเรียน**จากประเทศไทย** **ในชั้นเรียนของฉัน** **ที่มีกระเป๋าสีแดง**

## Common Thai Student Mistakes
1. **Omitting prepositions**: "I go school" instead of "I go to school"
2. **Using ที่ for everything**: "I live ที่ Monday" — confusing location ที่ with time
3. **Wrong preposition choice**: "I'm good in math" instead of "I'm good at math"
4. **Literal translation of Thai patterns**: "I go play" (ไปเล่น) instead of "I go to play"
