# Passive Voice: was/were + V3 → ถูก (thùuk) / โดน (dohn)

## Overview
Passive voice is one of the trickiest constructions for Thai students because Thai uses passive differently. In Thai, passive (ถูก/โดน) often implies something NEGATIVE happened to the subject. English passive is neutral.

## Key Differences

### English: Passive is neutral and common
- "The cake **was eaten** by the children." (neutral)
- "The report **was written** by the manager." (neutral, formal)
- "The window **was broken**." (could be accident)

### Thai: ถูก/โดน implies NEGATIVE experience
- เค้ก**ถูก**เด็กๆ กิน → sounds like the cake suffered from being eaten
- รายงาน**ถูก**เขียนโดยผู้จัดการ → sounds awkward in Thai
- หน้าต่าง**โดน**ทุบ → the window was hit/broken (negative)

### When Thai uses passive naturally:
- โดนตี (was hit) — negative
- ถูกจับ (was caught/arrested) — negative
- โดนด่า (was scolded) — negative
- ถูกไล่ออก (was fired) — negative

### When Thai avoids passive:
- "The letter was sent yesterday" → Thai prefers active: "เขาส่งจดหมายเมื่อวาน" (They sent the letter yesterday)
- "English is spoken worldwide" → Thai uses topic-comment: "ภาษาอังกฤษ คนทั่วโลกพูด"

## Pattern Mapping

| English Passive | Thai Approach | Notes |
|----------------|--------------|-------|
| Subject + was/were + V3 + by Agent | Subject + ถูก/โดน + Agent + Verb | Only for negative events |
| Subject + was/were + V3 (no agent) | Active rewrite preferred | ใครเป็นคนทำ? (Who did it?) |
| Subject + is being + V3 | กำลังถูก + Verb | Ongoing passive (rare in Thai) |
| Subject + has been + V3 | ถูก...แล้ว or ถูก...ไปแล้ว | Completed passive |

## Chunking Rules for Passive Voice
- "was/were" + past participle = ONE chunk
- Label: `verb`
- "by + agent" = separate chunk, label: `prepositional`
- The core SVO reconstruction should identify the TRUE actor (the "by" agent becomes the subject in Thai logic)

## Reconstruction in Thai Logic
English: "The exam was given by the professor."
1. Core: "The professor gave the exam" (active rewrite)
2. Thai: อาจารย์ให้ข้อสอบ (Professor + gave + exam)
3. NOT: ข้อสอบถูกให้โดยอาจารย์ (sounds unnatural)

## Common Thai Student Mistakes
1. **Using ถูก for positive events**: "I was promoted" → "ฉันถูกเลื่อนตำแหน่ง" (sounds like punishment)
2. **Translating passive literally**: Every "was + V3" gets ถูก/โดน treatment
3. **Missing the true subject**: Not recognizing who actually performed the action
