# Common Thai Student Mistakes in English

## Category 1: Word Order Errors

### Adjective Placement
- ❌ "house big" → ✅ "big house"
- Cause: Thai places adjectives AFTER nouns (บ้านใหญ่ = house + big)
- Frequency: Very high, especially beginners

### Question Formation
- ❌ "You go where?" → ✅ "Where do you go?"
- Cause: Thai questions keep the same word order (คุณไปไหน = you + go + where)
- Thai doesn't invert subject-verb for questions

### Negative Placement
- ❌ "I not go" → ✅ "I don't go"
- Cause: Thai negation is a simple prefix ไม่ (mâi) before the verb (ฉันไม่ไป)
- English requires auxiliary verb (do/does/did) for negation

## Category 2: Tense Errors

### No Past Tense Marking
- ❌ "Yesterday I go to school" → ✅ "Yesterday I went to school"
- Cause: Thai verb forms NEVER change for tense. เมื่อวานฉัน**ไป**โรงเรียน
- Thai uses time words (yesterday, tomorrow) instead of verb changes

### Present Perfect Confusion
- ❌ "I already eat" → ✅ "I have already eaten"
- Cause: Thai uses แล้ว (láew = already) without changing the verb: ฉันกินแล้ว

### Continuous Tense
- ❌ "I reading book" → ✅ "I am reading a book"
- Cause: Thai uses กำลัง (gam-lang) before verb without "to be": ฉันกำลังอ่านหนังสือ

## Category 3: Article Errors

### Missing Articles
- ❌ "I am student" → ✅ "I am a student"
- ❌ "Sun is hot" → ✅ "The sun is hot"
- Cause: Thai has NO articles. There is no equivalent of a/an/the.

### This is the MOST common error for Thai students at all levels.
- Rules of thumb for Thai students:
  - Use "a/an" for "one of many" (a student = นักเรียนคนหนึ่ง)
  - Use "the" for "the specific one" (the student = นักเรียนคนนั้น)
  - Use "the" for "the only one" (the sun, the moon)

## Category 4: Pronoun/Subject Errors

### Subject Dropping
- ❌ "Is very good" → ✅ "It is very good"
- Cause: Thai is a pro-drop language — subjects are often omitted: ดีมาก (very good)

### Gender Confusion
- ❌ "My sister, he is..." → ✅ "My sister, she is..."
- Cause: Thai third-person pronoun เขา (khǎo) is gender-neutral

## Category 5: Preposition Errors

### Using ที่ for Everything
- ❌ "I arrive at Bangkok" → ✅ "I arrive in Bangkok"
- ❌ "I interest in science" → ✅ "I am interested in science"
- Cause: Thai ที่ covers in/on/at, so students can't distinguish English prepositions

### Omitting Prepositions
- ❌ "I go school" → ✅ "I go to school"
- Cause: Thai often omits prepositions: ไปโรงเรียน (go + school)

## Category 6: Connector Errors

### Double Conjunctions
- ❌ "Although it rained, but I went out" → ✅ "Although it rained, I went out"
- Cause: Thai uses paired connectors: แม้ว่า...แต่ (although...but)

### Because + So
- ❌ "Because I was tired, so I slept" → ✅ "Because I was tired, I slept"
- Cause: Thai uses เพราะ...เลย (because...so) as a pair

## Impact on LinguBreak Chunking
When analyzing sentences, be aware:
1. Thai students may submit sentences with these errors — handle gracefully
2. The "Thai Logic" reconstruction should highlight WHY the English structure differs from Thai
3. Common error patterns should be mentioned in thai_explanation when relevant
